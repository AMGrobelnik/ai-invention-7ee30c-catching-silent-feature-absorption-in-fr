=== (A) M2' SAFETY-RELEVANT IDENTITY-ABSORPTION POSITIONING ===

The conjunction to defend is the explicit novelty boundary: a single SAFETY/IDENTITY sub-context (a demographic/dialect/identity token absorbed under a general identity parent) is (1) DISCOVERED label-free as one absorber latent by interventional two-track grouping; (2) EDITED to remove/recover only that sub-context while PRESERVING the parent identity concept and the sibling sub-contexts on the same hierarchy; and (3) SCORED against the STRONGEST dense baseline -- a SUB-CONTEXT-targeted difference-of-means direction u_sub = diff-of-means(target-sub-context-positive, sibling-positive) -- at matched on-target (forget) effect on a joint on-target/collateral/fluency metric. No surveyed method combines all three.

Five sub-literatures were surveyed. (1) SAE-based debiasing edits or suppresses WHOLE bias-correlated features, often in non-LLM modalities: debiaSAE [1] benchmarks and debiases bias features in vision-language models (under review at COLM 2025); DeBiasLens [4] deactivates per-group 'social-attribute neurons' in VLMs (CVPR 2026 per its page, flagged); SteerRM [3] suppresses whole stylistic-bias SAE features in a reward model at inference, explicitly noting 'direct activation suppression degrades performance due to representation entanglement'; and the closest near-miss, Ahsan & Wallace [2] (camera-ready ICLR 2026), steers a SINGLE race-correlated SAE latent in clinical LLMs -- but that latent CO-FIRES (activates on 'African American' AND 'incarceration'), i.e. it is entangled, not an absorber with a clean parent recall-hole, and the paper concedes that 'mitigating bias via SAE steering appears to be of marginal utility for realistic tasks' [2]. (2) Model-editing for stereotype edits a WHOLE stereotype association: BiasEdit [5] (TrustNLP @ NAACL 2025) uses lightweight editor networks to edit partial LM parameters on StereoSet/Crows-Pairs; Collapsed-LMs [6] (ICLR 2025) promotes fairness via neural-collapse fine-tuning. Neither discovers an SAE absorber or compares against u_sub. (3) Fairness/concept-erasure editing ablates a WHOLE dense protected-attribute direction while preserving utility: Karvonen & Marks [7] (NeurIPS 2025 Mech-Interp Workshop) apply an affine inference-time edit to race/gender directions, 'consistently reducing bias to very low levels (typically under 1%, always below 2.5%) while largely maintaining model performance'; SPLINCE [8] (NeurIPS 2025) removes a linear concept 'while exactly preserving [its] covariance with a target label', proven the unique minimal-distortion solution; H-SAL [9] erases latent concepts from textual profiles WITHOUT protected-attribute labels and finds 'implicit self-description often matches or outperforms explicit-label-based debiasing'. These are the whole-concept dense move CCRG improves on with a sub-context u_sub (and double as WS2 evidence below). (4) Identity/entity/PII unlearning removes a WHOLE entity/record and preserves UNRELATED knowledge: Entity-Level Unlearning [10] (COLING 2025) erases entity-related knowledge completely; Not Every Token Needs Forgetting [11] selectively unlearns a critical token subset while preserving the retain set; DFSU [12] removes token-level PII via a LoRA subspace. None is feature-discovered or operates on a same-hierarchy parent/sibling structure. (5) Label-free worst-group robustness by reweighting -- JTT [19], GEORGE [20], EIIL [21], LfF [22] -- infers GROUPS OVER EXAMPLES and RETRAINS with a reweighted/group-DRO loss; CCRG groups FEATURES, never retrains, and the recovered absorber is itself the auditable, editable sub-context specialist -- an orthogonal route.

Three adversarial disprove searches ('SAE single-feature edit one demographic sub-context preserve parent identity concept'; 'discovered SAE latent beats dense diff-of-means at sub-context removal preserving parent'; 'feature absorption on demographic/identity attribute with suppressed parent') returned only whole-attribute SAE debiasing, image-editing methods, and the entangled single-latent near-miss [2] -- NO precedent for the conjunction. Crucially, the same searches found NO documented feature absorption on safety/identity attributes with a suppressed parent and recall hole; the documented absorption literature remains first-letter spelling plus Matryoshka's 'Lily/all-female-names-except-Lily' name example. This directly supports the honest-null branch: identity attributes tend to be co-firing/entangled (as [2] shows), not absorption-structured.

Both-branches framing (GEN_PAPER_TEXT keeps whichever the experiments produced): BRANCH-WIN -- a discovered absorber edit that, at matched forget, preserves the parent and siblings better than u_sub converts auditability into task performance on a safety-relevant sub-context, distinct from whole-group erasure [10]-[12] and from reweighting debiasing [19]-[22]. BRANCH-NULL -- if no safety attribute is absorption-structured (the parent has no recall hole, so there is no single absorber to name and edit), bound the safety contribution to the auditable repair/edit PRIMITIVE (demonstrated on the homograph/spelling sub-contexts), connect it to the existing 0/28-professions and toxicity-co-firing negatives, and cite [2]'s entanglement + marginal-utility finding as corroboration. GUARDRAIL: do not claim to be the first SAE debiasing method ([1]-[4] precede it); claim only the conjunction.

=== (B) M1' u_sub LABEL-EFFICIENCY POSITIONING + RETIREMENT OF THE FALSE ARGUMENT ===

The iter-5 draft argued that 'a single dense hyperplane structurally cannot localize to a sub-context' and that 'erasing the is-a-country direction removes all countries'. With the comparator changed to u_sub = diff-of-means(target-sub-context-positive, sibling-positive within the same parent context), this is FALSE: u_sub IS a single dense hyperplane and DOES localize to the sub-context (it points along the target-vs-sibling contrast inside the parent, so removing it need not touch the parent or siblings), and the testbed already carries the per-sub-context labels that define u_sub. External evidence that a correctly-targeted dense direction localizes and preserves utility: SPLINCE [8] removes a linear concept while exactly preserving target-label covariance; Karvonen & Marks [7] reduce bias below 2.5% while maintaining performance via an affine edit; H-SAL [9] shows label-free erasure matches label-based debiasing. Therefore any M1' win cannot come from 'dense can't localize'. The report supplies an exact DELETE/REPLACE list (remove every 'structurally cannot localize', 'removes all countries', 'dense over-shoots by construction', and any LEACE-as-decisive-comparator framing; keep whole-parent (f)/LEACE only as a clearly-labeled SECONDARY 'naive over-shoot' reference, never the headline comparator).

The defensible claim if KG-ABL only MATCHES u_sub is a LABEL-EFFICIENCY / DISCOVERY claim: the absorber is discovered label-free by interventional grouping, whereas u_sub REQUIRES the sub-context partition (labeled target/sibling sets). This is grounded in Peng et al.'s position paper 'Use Sparse Autoencoders to Discover Unknown Concepts, Not to Act on Known Concepts' [13], whose verbatim thesis (grep-confirmed) is 'while SAEs may be less effective for acting on known concepts, SAEs are powerful tools for discovering unknown concepts', with concept detection and model steering classified as 'acting on known concepts' (where SAEs underperform) and discovery framed as 'enumerating concepts in an unsupervised manner'. CCRG is the constructive instantiation -- the grouping DISCOVERS the sub-context handle a labeled dense direction would otherwise need supervision to find -- which RECONCILES rather than is defeated by the threat. The general label-free principle (SAE latents are obtained with no concept supervision) is reinforced by 'Beyond Interpretability: ...Label-Free Visual Steering' [14], which trains a top-k SAE on unlabeled activations and steers without labels (modality note: vision; cite for the principle).

Both M1' fork paragraphs are supplied. FORK-WIN: a discovered single SAE feature beats even a sub-context-labeled dense direction at parent-preserving sub-context removal, at matched forget, under an identical LLM judge with KL/PPL corroboration -- the strong contribution, distinct from all surveyed methods (none compares a discovered single absorber against a sub-context-targeted dense baseline), scoped to the single-sub-context-with-parent-preservation regime, conceding dense wins on aggregate whole-concept steering [16]. FORK-MATCH: KG-ABL matches u_sub WITHOUT requiring the sub-context partition -- the label-efficiency/discovery contribution, citing Peng [13] and label-free SAE [14],[9], conceding u_sub is competitive given labels (consistent with the AxBench/DeepMind dense-beats-SAE picture [16]). HONEST COST NOTE: label efficiency is relative, not free -- interventional grouping consumes content counterfactual pairs (an unlabeled-but-not-free signal); the honest comparison is counterfactual-pair cost (grouping) vs sub-context-partition-label cost (u_sub).
